Our Heritage

Eight Decades of Flavor

In 1945, brothers-in-law Burt Baskin and Irv Robbins combined their passion for ice cream with a revolutionary idea: a store that offered 31 flavors — one for every day of the month.

"We sell fun, not just ice cream," Irv Robbins famously said. That philosophy transformed a single Glendale, California shop into the world's largest chain of ice cream specialty stores.

Today, Baskin-Robbins continues that legacy of innovation, crafting new flavor experiences while honoring the classics that started it all.

1945 Irv Robbins opens Snowbird Ice Cream in Glendale, CA. Burt Baskin opens Burton's Ice Cream nearby.
1953 The two shops merge into Baskin-Robbins, introducing the iconic "31 Flavors" concept.
1970 Goes international — first stores open in Japan and other global markets.
2022 Major brand refresh with new logo, "Seize the Yay" tagline, and modernized store experience.
